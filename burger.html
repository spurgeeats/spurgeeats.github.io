We don’t often order in. Perhaps a handful of times in the past seven months. That being said, this order stood out. It had a certain jenny say quan...the guac..the boeuf..the truffle fries…the fried pickles….the sheer size of the thing. It was good. Okay, the truffle fries weren’t actually that great. I had overhyped it, as I tend to do. As punishment my chicken tendies weren’t as amazing as I had remembered. However, when we walked into the establishment to pick up our order, you exclaimed that there were “a lot of Hasidic Jews in here”. My theory is that they overheard this and gave me the bad tendies. For the purposes of this recipe, though, I am dropping the tendies, the pickles, and the fries, and focusing on how to recreate the burger. We must do things deliberately.

burger:

85% lean ground beef

lettuce

burger buns


green chili relish:

onion, chopped

olive oil

green chilies, drained and chopped

dried oregano

cilantro, chopped

salt, to taste

To prepare, begin by shaping those patties. I usually go for 85% ground beef because more fat = more yum. That being said, use what moves you. Put the patties to the side, begin heating your cast iron, and make the guac. Mash those avocados, chop up the stuff that needs to be chopped, combine it all, and maybe try a little bit with some chips because why not. In all honestly, I pulled the green chili relish from an online recipe but it seems accurate enough. Combine all that stuff in a bowl until it tastes yum - perhaps try sautéing the onions for the relish as well.


Put those patties on heat. I go for medium rare, but this is controversial in the black community so again, do what moves you. Toast the buns. Put the buns, patties, lettuce, relish, and guac together in whatever order feels right. There are no rules because I did not ask for the recipe from the restaurant.
