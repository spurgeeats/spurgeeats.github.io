arrabbiata sauce:

cherry tomatoes, sliced

tomato paste

pancetta, cubed

chili pepper flakes

basil, torn

shallots, thinly sliced

olive oil

salt and pep, to taste

Begin by preparing the flour. It’s a simple mixture of flour, water, and eggs. Try using the well technique - making a pyramid of flour and carving a well for the egg-water mixture to go into, and then gently mixing everything together. Knead, knead, knead, until the dough is stretchy and slightly sticky. Place the dough ball into a bowl and cover with a tea towel for an hour. Meanwhile, make the spinach-ricotta filling.


To do so, clean the spinach and blanch for about 30 seconds. Squeeze out the water, and chop finely. Try to drain the ricotta from as much water as possible, also. This can get messy, but soggy filling will break the pasta. Add grated parmesan, an egg, and some spices to thicken the mixture. Taste along the way if you’re not afraid of salmonella.


Once the dough has risen, use the pasta maker to make long thin strips - not too thick, as it’ll take forever to cook and will be too chewy to eat (lesson learned, lol). Use a glass cup to make circular cutouts of pasta, continuously dusting the work surface with flour so things don’t stick. Spoon spinach-ricotta filing into the center of the circles, and then use a bit of water to seal the edges. Once everything is prepared, begin boiling some salted water to cook. Refrigerate while you’re waiting.


Now for the sauce. Toast the garlic, chili flakes, and shallots in a generous amount of olive oil in a pan on medium. Try not to burn. Remove and add the pancetta and cook until most of the fat has rendered. Remove the pancetta, re-add the garlic/shallot mix, and add the cherry tomatoes. Add the tomato paste, allowing it to cook down until the red color has darkened. Now is a good time to put the ravioli in the water - it’ll take about 10 minutes. Add some of the salted pasta water to the sauce, and cook things back down again. Add heavy cream, in dashes and tasting along the way, as well as some fresh basil. Combine it all on a plate once the ravioli is done, grate some cheese on top, and eat!


This is a once a year type recipe, if you couldn’t tell.
